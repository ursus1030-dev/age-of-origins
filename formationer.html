Formations

How to stack your march so the right troops take the right hits.

← Back to Combat

The basics

A formation in Age of Origins is the mix of troops you send. One backbone works for almost every attack: one soldier of each type, plus the maximum of snipers and long-range cannons you can field. Everything else you add or drop depending on the job.

This same backbone is what you use for a synchronized attack (SA), and for a solo SOS run.

When to bring what

Start from the backbone, then adjust for the job in front of you.

Fake attack

One soldier of each type — nothing more. It looks like a march without committing real force, so you can bait a reaction or scout without risking troops.

Group attack

The full backbone: one of each type plus max snipers and long-range cannons. This is the standard rally march.

Solo attack

Backbone plus staying power: 2,000–5,000 shredders scaled to the enemy's strength, as many zombies as you can bring, and lasers depending on their level.

Common mistakes

  • Sending a solo march at an online player without speedups ready — you can't pull out of a trap in time.
  • Hitting a city while its troops are out — you spend a full march on an empty target.
  • Pouring reinforcements into what's actually a fake attack — check for a titan or a plane first; if neither is there, it's bait.
  • Committing a rally before checking fog — probe first so you know how many defenders are really there.
